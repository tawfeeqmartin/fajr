## Interpretation

The simplified Meeus chapter 25 solar series is published with ~1' (60″) longitude accuracy for modern dates. Targets here reflect that: ≤60″ in RA/Dec and ≤0.01% in distance.

Solar position is the second primitive (alongside lunar) feeding hilal classification — this validation closes the primitives-validation half of the hilal accuracy chain. For hilal specifically only RA/Dec matter (used to compute the geocentric elongation arc with the Moon); solar distance does not enter the calculation.

- RA  max-abs: 14.78″ — ✅ within target
- Dec max-abs: 9.64″ — ✅ within target
- Distance max-abs: 0.0039% — ✅ within target

Light-time correction (~8 min for the Sun at 1 AU ≈ 0.005° apparent shift) is applied by JPL's astrometric quantity but not by `solarPosition()`. Negligible at this scale.
