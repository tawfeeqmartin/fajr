## Verdict

**REJECTED — but for an instructive reason.**

`eval/compare.js` failed the change with:

```
fajr: bias shifted -0.42 min — calc moved EARLIER (cuts into prayer time), violates ihtiyat.
```

The compare.js encodes a single direction of Fajr ihtiyat: *later is safe (prayer-only)*. By that rule, moving Fajr earlier is unsafe. But:

- The Ramadan-fasting ihtiyat is the **opposite** — earlier is safe.
- Imsak (fasting) and Fajr (prayer) start times are both derived from the same dawn calculation, but they have **opposite ihtiyat polarities**.
- The user's stated concern is fasting-safety. The encoded ratchet rule is prayer-only.
- The change moved the engine *toward* mosque-published reality (independently confirmed by Mawaqit), so the −0.42 min Fajr bias drift is *not* a quality regression — it is the ratchet's prayer-only assumption breaking down.

This is a **policy gap** in `eval/compare.js`, not an engine quality issue. The change is reverted to honor the existing ratchet contract; the policy decision is escalated to the human (Tawfeeq).
