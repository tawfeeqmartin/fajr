# v1.7.5 city-registry — Reviewer C (edge-case stress)

## Methodology

Direct invocation of `detectLocation(lat, lon)` over **308 deliberately-hard
coordinates** picked across seven adversarial categories: high-density Muslim
sub-coords (CBD/airport/suburb), multi-bbox metros, border cities,
dispute zones, tiny states, ocean/Antarctica, and sub-national admin
overlaps. Each call captured the returned `city.name`, `city.countryISO`,
top-level `country` (from `detectCountry`), `recommendedMethod`, and
`source.{type, institution|from}`. Cross-checked the 13 entries that
showed `city.countryISO !== country` (the silent-mismatch class). For
dispute zones I applied a single principle: **fajr is a positional
library, so dispatch should follow the user's actual GPS position
mapped to the institution that the local Muslim community follows —
not the political-recognition layer above it.** I do *not* take a
position on which sovereign claims a territory; I check whether the
institutional method that fires at that coord matches the
locally-followed timetable.

Test scripts are at `/tmp/test_detect.mjs`, `/tmp/test_detect2.mjs`,
`/tmp/test_detect3.mjs`, `/tmp/test_detect4.mjs` (gitignored).

## Key issues (severity-ranked)

### 🟥 Definitely wrong (engineer-fixable)

1. **Canada → USA across S Canadian belt**. Toronto, Montreal, Quebec City, Ottawa, Mississauga, Niagara Falls ON, Windsor — all return country="USA" / method=ISNA. Root cause: USA bbox lat 24-50 lon -125 to -66 fully contains S Canada south of 50°N, and USA is checked BEFORE Canada (line 209 vs 286 in `engine.js`). Canada must be moved BEFORE USA, OR Canada bbox latitude minimum must equal USA's latitude maximum + the engine must rely on Canada being checked first. **Affects ~30M Canadians**, the third-largest Western Muslim community.

2. **Singapore registry-bbox is too large; Johor Bahru bbox overlaps**. Both Singapore (`[0.95-1.75, 103.42-104.22]`) and Johor Bahru (`[1.29-1.69, 103.54-103.94]`) cover central Singapore. JB has smaller area → wins. Result: Singapore CBD coord (1.35, 103.82) returns matched-city `Johor Bahru` while country `Singapore`. **Sub-national display broken for Singapore proper**.

3. **Saudi Arabia bbox swallows Egyptian Red Sea coast** (Sinai resorts incl. Sharm el-Sheikh, Marsa Alam). Saudi's bbox `lon 34-56` overlaps Egypt's `lon 24-38` at the 34-38 strip. Saudi is checked first (line 79 vs Egypt at 119). Egyptian Sinai dispatches to UmmAlQura instead of Egyptian (19.5°/17.5°). **Different prayer times**. Affects Sharm, Hurghada area, St Catherine, Marsa Alam.

4. **Saudi N border (Hafar al-Batin) → Iran / Tehran**. Iran bbox `lat 25-39 lon 44-63` overlaps Saudi's `lat 16-33 lon 34-56` at lat 25-33 lon 44-56. Iran is checked first. Hafar al-Batin (28.43, 45.97) and any Saudi NE town (lon ≥44) → Iran / Tehran. **Wrong method** for Saudi residents.

## Recommendations for the implementer

In rough priority order (1 = top impact):

1. **Reorder country bbox checks** in `detectCountry`. Specifically:
   - Move **Canada before USA** (or shrink USA's lat max to 49 — the actual border).
   - Move **Egypt before Saudi Arabia** (or split Egypt's bbox to `lat 21-32 lon 24-38` AND ensure Saudi starts at lon 34.8+ to leave Sinai to Egypt; current Saudi `lon 34` swallows Sinai).
   - Move **Saudi Arabia before Iran** (Iran's Persian Gulf shore should be lon 50+, but it currently extends to 44 and swallows Saudi Eastern Province).
   - Move **Russia far earlier** — at minimum before Finland (currently Russia is *last* in detectCountry and Finland's `lat 59-71 lon 19-32` swallows St Petersburg; same for Poland and Kaliningrad).
   - Move **Ukraine after Belarus** so Belarusian Brest doesn't go to Ukraine.
   - Move **China before India and before Kyrgyzstan** for the Tibet / Xinjiang strip — OR shrink India's `lon 68-97.4` to stop at China's western edge.
   - Move **Italy before Austria** so Bolzano doesn't go to Austria.
   - Move **Spain's lat max from 42.15 to 43.79** to include Basque country and N. Spain.
   - Move **Morocco's lat min from 27 to 21** to include Western Sahara south (Dakhla) — OR keep current and accept Western Sahara dispatch to Mauritania as a design call.

2. **Fix overlapping registry-city bboxes**:
   - Singapore bbox is too large (extends to lat 0.95 = southern Bintan/Batam). Trim to actual Singapore island: `[1.16, 1.47, 103.6, 104.05]`.
   - Johor Bahru bbox overlaps Singapore proper. Trim south edge to lat 1.43 (above Singapore's island).
   - Sharjah bbox overlaps Dubai. Trim south edge of Sharjah to lat 25.20 (above Dubai's centre).
   - Dearborn bbox extends to lat 42.42 north of the Detroit River; Windsor (Canada) at 42.31 falls inside. Trim Dearborn east edge or apply countryISO check: if user's country (per detectCountry) ≠ city's countryISO, don't match.

3. **Add a city/country sanity check**: after city match, if `city.countryISO` doesn't agree with `detectCountry`'s result, prefer the smaller bbox of the two layers, OR flag the mismatch and fall back to country-only dispatch. Cleaner: for each city in the registry, verify all four bbox corners resolve to the same country in detectCountry; reject the registry entry if not.

4. **Add Cyprus, Andorra, Monaco, Liechtenstein, Vatican, San Marino bboxes** — even if all dispatch to fallback methods, having explicit `country` strings (e.g. 'Cyprus', 'Andorra') lets downstream apps render correct labels.

5. **Add a Tibet/Xinjiang-specific dispatch** (China, Hanafi-cluster) to differentiate from Han China where appropriate. Current China-default MCM is fine for the Han majority but Uyghur/Hui communities may follow different conventions — at minimum make sure the country label says "China" for Lhasa and Kashgar.

6. **Add Hawaii/Alaska/PR/Guam** to USA bbox or as separate USA-territory bboxes. Or document that they're explicitly fallback.

7. **Consider Sinai-specific override**: Egypt's Sinai (lon 32-34, lat 27-31.5) has unique fiqh convention (Bedouin tradition + Saudi influence at coast). Probably overkill — fixing Saudi/Egypt order is enough.

8. **Add a non-binary `disputed` flag** to detectLocation output for areas where country attribution is genuinely ambiguous (Kashmir, Crimea, NorthernCyprus, WesternSahara, Falklands, etc.). Lets downstream apps surface "Disputed: PK admin / IN claim" rather than picking one side silently.

9. **Coverage gaps to fix in Reviewer A's bbox audit**: my run found that `London`, `Paris`, `Tokyo` city bboxes are unusually large (cover multiple counties / prefectures). If Reviewer A is testing bbox geometry uniformly, they may flag these as oversized. They're actually correct for what users want — but consider adding `metroBbox` distinct from `cityBbox` so airports outside the city proper still match.

10. **Document the priority order**: every fix above is a bbox-order edit. Add a docstring at the top of detectCountry explaining "bbox checks run top-to-bottom; smaller / more specific OR border-zone country must come first" and reference this audit.
